Quarterly Science Exhibit

Primary pupils and other stakeholders were in awe as they witnessed the amazing outputs of intermediate learners for the 3rd Quarter in Science. Under Project SAIL (Science Amazing Innovations for Learning), a quarterly science exhibit must be held. This 3rd Quarter a science exhibit was set up to showcase the outputs of the intermediate learners. The activity showed what science skills the learners gained in the said quarter.

The activity not only highlighted the students' academic achievements but also fostered a sense of curiosity and excitement for science among the attendees. Visitors marveled at the creativity and ingenuity displayed by the intermediate learners, who enthusiastically presented their projects and experiments.

Leading the charge was Madam Gwyneth A. Payumo, an esteemed intermediate science teacher and science district area specialist. With her expertise and passion for science education, Madam Payumo guided students in conceptualizing, designing, and executing their projects, ensuring that each exhibit was not only educational but also engaging and interactive.

The success of the project/activity was made possible through the unwavering support and approval of Dr. Mirasol T. Valencia, ESHT III of JNES. Dr. Valencia's dedication to promoting innovative learning experiences and her commitment to academic excellence were instrumental in bringing the science exhibit to fruition.

Catch Up Friday

"Catching Up for Updates on Catch Up Fridays" is an engaging elementary school activity designed to help students stay informed and connected with their peers and teachers. Typically held on Fridays, this activity provides students with an opportunity to reflect on their week, share experiences, and celebrate achievements.

During "Catching Up for Updates," students might gather in a circle or virtual meeting space where each child takes turns sharing highlights from their week. This could include accomplishments in academics, sports, arts, or personal goals they've achieved. Teachers may also use this time to provide updates on upcoming events, assignments, or important information for the following week.

The activity promotes a sense of community and belonging within the classroom, fostering positive relationships between students and their teachers. It encourages active listening, empathy, and support for one another's successes and challenges. Additionally, "Catch Up Fridays" can serve as a platform for students to practice communication skills, build confidence, and develop social-emotional competencies in a supportive environment. Overall, it's a fun and inclusive way to wrap up the week and set a positive tone for the days ahead.
